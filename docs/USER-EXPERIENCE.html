StartDashboard

A personal home page that travels with you across every browser and every device, with separate "channels" for different sides of your life.

Last updated: 2026-05-29 · v1.14
01

What This App Does

The problem this app solves and the basic experience of using it.

StartDashboard replaces the fractured pile of bookmarks you accumulate across Chrome, Safari, and every other browser you touch. It's one web page, at one URL, that looks and behaves identically wherever you open it. You bookmark a site once and it shows up everywhere — on your laptop, your phone, your work computer — within seconds.

It also recognizes that you're not one person to your browser. You have a work mode, a personal mode, and probably a few obsessions besides (investments, side projects, things you're learning). Each of those is a "persona": its own complete dashboard with its own bookmarks, its own widgets, its own background, and its own accent color. You switch between them with one click on a tab bar at the top of the page.

The default aesthetic is dark, modern, and tech-leaning — think Linear, Vercel, Raycast. Bookmark categories sit on top of a chosen background (animated gradients, deep space, a synthwave grid, or any image URL you paste in). A sun/moon button in the header flips the whole UI to a clean light mode for daytime / bright-light use, and each browser remembers which theme you picked. A small row of widgets near the top gives you a multi-time-zone clock, the weather, a to-do list, and a sticky notes pad — one set per persona, so your work to-dos and personal to-dos never mix.

Behind the scenes, all your data lives in one private GitHub gist on your account. There are no ads, no third-party services besides GitHub and a free weather API, and nothing tracks what you do.

Key Features

The major features, what they let users do, and how they work in practice.

Personas (channels)

Multiple complete dashboards under one URL.

A tab bar across the top shows your personas — Work, Personal, Investments, anything you create. One click switches the whole dashboard: different bookmarks, different widgets, different background. Each persona is fully independent. Add a new persona with the + button; rename by double-clicking a tab; reorder by dragging; right-click for delete and "make default on this device."

Ask Gemini — AI chat without leaving the dashboard (v1.11)

A floating bubble opens a chat panel for quick, search-style answers.

A floating chat bubble sits in the bottom-right corner on every persona. Click it and a panel slides over from the right. Type a question, press Enter, and you get a concise answer — the assistant is tuned for short, direct, search-style replies rather than long essays, and it remembers your recent back-and-forth for follow-ups. It’s powered by Google’s free Gemini API: generate a key at aistudio.google.com/app/apikey (no credit card) and paste it into Settings once per browser. A header “Open full Gemini ↗” button jumps to gemini.google.com when you want your real, logged-in Gemini account. Note: this in-dashboard chat is a separate, fresh assistant — it doesn’t see your personal Gemini history; the link-out is for that. Your chat here is saved on this device (not synced to your other devices), and “Clear chat” wipes it. If you haven’t added a key yet, the panel shows a one-line prompt; if Gemini is ever unreachable, you get a short note with the link-out rather than an error. If Google’s servers are briefly overloaded (a “high demand” blip on Google’s side), the chat quietly retries on its own and usually recovers without you seeing anything — and if it can’t, it shows a plain-English note with a one-click Retry button (v1.13).

Bookmarks with three add paths

Add a bookmark however feels natural in the moment.

The bookmarklet lives in your browser's bookmarks bar — one click on any site sends it to your dashboard. Or drag a URL straight from the address bar onto a category card. Or click + Add bookmark at the bottom of any category and paste a URL by hand.

Bookmark descriptions on hover

Add your own notes without cluttering the row.

Click the 📝 icon on any bookmark to open a multi-line editor with room for reminders, asterisks, line breaks — whatever you'd jot down. The description stays hidden by default; an accent dot next to the title signals that there's more to read. Hover over the bookmark for half a second and a popover appears with the full description text.

Inline rename, drag-to-reorder with grip handles

Organize without ever leaving the page.

Double-click anything — persona tab, category title, bookmark name — to rename. To reorder, grab the ⠿ grip handle (as of v1.9, every draggable item shows one) and drag; a blue line shows where the item will land when you let go. This works for categories, bookmarks, the lists inside the widget config (your stock tickers, weather cities, and clock zones), and — new in v1.10 — the widget cards themselves: grab the grip in any widget’s header and drag it left or right to set the order you want. You can drag a bookmark within its category or drop it into a different category. Use the ↗ icon on a bookmark to move it to a different persona entirely.

Per-persona widgets — show, hide, configure

Each persona shows only the widgets it needs.

The available widgets: Clock (multi time zone, 12 or 24 hour), Weather (free Open-Meteo, no key needed — as of v1.12 each forecast day shows a condition icon + short label and a chance-of-rain percentage alongside the high/low), To-do list, Sticky notes, Crypto (free CoinGecko, no key), Markets (S&P, Nasdaq, Dow via Finnhub — needs a free API key), and one or more Watchlists (your custom stocks, also Finnhub — as of v1.10 you can have several, each with its own title). Each persona picks which widgets to show via Settings ⚙, and (new in v1.10) which order they appear in by dragging their cards. An AI Education persona can have zero widgets; an Investments persona might show only Markets, Crypto, and a couple of watchlists.

Multiple watchlists, each with its own title (v1.10)

Group your stocks however makes sense — not just one big list.

You used to have a single Watchlist. As of v1.10 you can have as many as you like — say “Tech,” “Dividends,” and “Watching,” each its own card with its own stocks. Add one with the “+ Add watchlist” button in Settings (just below the show/hide-widgets toggles). Each watchlist’s ⚙ gear lets you rename it (the title updates as you type), add or remove tickers, and delete the whole list (with a confirm so you don’t lose it by accident). Your old single watchlist carries over automatically as the first one. Markets stays a single card — it’s the index view (S&P/Nasdaq/Dow), separate from your personal lists.

Per-card border and glow color (v1.10)

Tint any card to make it stand out, with a one-click reset.

Give any category card or widget card its own border and soft glow color. For a category, open its “...” menu and choose “Card color…”. For a widget, open its ⚙ gear and use the “Card color” section (the To-do and Notes widgets gained a small gear just for this). Either way you get preset swatches plus a custom color picker. The glow is subtle and doesn’t move. To clear it, pick “Default (theme)” and the card goes back to the persona’s accent color. Color is per-persona, so the same category can look different in Work vs Personal.

Finance widgets show dollar and percent change

Daily moves at a glance, in both formats.

Crypto, Markets, and Watchlist rows all show the current price plus today's change in both dollars and percent. Green if up, red if down, color-coded for fast scanning. As of v1.9, Markets and Watchlist are powered by Finnhub (free, 60 calls/min) instead of Twelve Data — this fixed the rate-limit errors that used to appear when tracking a dozen-plus symbols. You generate a free Finnhub key at finnhub.io/register (no credit card) and paste it into Settings once per browser. Stock prices are cached for 5 minutes and shared across all your tabs, so opening the dashboard in several tabs doesn't re-fetch over and over. If a single ticker can't load it shows “—” rather than breaking the whole widget; if the rate limit is ever hit, you'll see “Rate limit reached — prices refresh shortly” and the widget quietly refreshes a minute later.

Custom backgrounds and accent colors

Make each persona feel distinct.

Choose from 10 built-in dark backgrounds (Midnight Mesh, Aurora, Synthwave, Carbon Grid, Deep Space, Liquid Glass, Tokyo Night, Terminal, Monochrome, and — new in v1.14 — Starlink) — all CSS-only, no images to load. Or paste any image URL (an Unsplash link works great). Pick an accent color and it colors the persona's tab indicator, hover states, and focus rings. Starlink is a calm, cinematic deep-space scene (near-black sky, faint stars, a soft Milky Way band, and blue/teal/purple nebula washes); picking it also auto-switches the persona's accent to a matched electric blue, which you can still override afterward.

Sync across every browser and device

Same dashboard everywhere, within seconds.

Your data lives in a private GitHub gist on your account. Edits save automatically. A small indicator in the header shows the sync status: green dot when in sync, amber when saving, red when another browser has updated it (click to reload). When the bookmarklet adds a bookmark to a different persona than you're viewing, a banner appears: "Just added X to Personal — Switch to Personal."

Per-device default persona

Your work Mac opens to Work. Your iPhone opens to Personal.

Each browser remembers which persona to load on startup. You set it during first-time setup or in Settings — as of v1.6 the "Default persona on this browser/device" block sits at the very top of Settings with a 🖥 icon, so you'll see it immediately. The default is per-device, so different machines can open to different personas without interfering with each other.

Light / dark mode toggle

One click in the header flips the whole UI between dark and light. (v1.6)

A sun/moon icon button sits in the header next to the gear. ☀ in dark mode (click to go light), 🌙 in light mode (click to go dark). Light mode swaps the persona's gradient background for a clean light surface, flips text and card colors for readability, and keeps the persona's accent color so tab indicators, hover states, and focus rings still feel like the right persona. Each browser remembers its own theme, so you can run Safari in light mode and Chrome in dark on the same Mac.

Built-in web search

Type and hit Enter — opens a search in a new tab.

A search bar lives below the header. Type a query and press Enter to search Google by default. In Settings you can swap to DuckDuckGo, Bing, or Kagi.

Screens & Views

The distinct screens of the app and what users can do on each.

Setup screen

Shown the first time you open the dashboard in any browser. A three-step form: paste your GitHub token, choose whether this is your first browser (create a fresh gist) or you're joining an existing setup (paste the gist ID from another browser), then continue. Disappears once setup is complete.

Main dashboard

What you see day-to-day. Header on top (title, persona tabs, sync indicator, theme toggle, settings button). Search bar below that. A row of widget cards (up to seven per persona, configurable). Categories grid below, each category a card containing bookmarks. Background is whatever this persona has selected.

Bookmarklet add overlay

What you see when you click the bookmarklet on a website. A small popup window opens with the URL and title pre-filled. You see all your personas grouped, and within each, buttons for every category. Click any button to add the bookmark there. The window auto-closes once saved.

Ask Gemini chat panel (v1.11)

A slide-over panel triggered by the floating chat bubble (bottom-right of the main dashboard). Header with the title, the model name, an “Open full Gemini ↗” link-out, and a close ✕; a scrollable message area below; an input row at the bottom; and a “Clear chat” footer. It overlays the dashboard but sits beneath the Settings/config modals, and works in both light and dark mode. On a phone it goes full-width.

Settings modal

Triggered by the gear icon in the header. The first block at the top is now a focal "Default persona on this browser/device" picker (🖥 icon, accent-tinted background, explanatory subtext) so per-device defaults are obvious instead of buried. Below that: dashboard title, this persona's name + accent color + background, clock zones, weather cities, search engine, gist ID (for copying to other browsers). Includes a Bookmarklet button that shows the drag-to-install link, and a Danger Zone block at the bottom for destructive actions (Delete persona, Reset This Browser).

Primary Workflows

Step-by-step walkthroughs of the most common things users do in this app.

Ask a quick question without leaving the dashboard (v1.11)

  1. Click the floating chat bubble in the bottom-right corner. A panel slides over from the right.
  2. Type your question and press Enter (Shift+Enter for a new line). A concise answer appears in the panel; ask follow-ups and it keeps the recent context.
  3. One-time: if you see “Add your free Gemini API key,” open Settings, generate a free key at aistudio.google.com/app/apikey, paste it in, and Save.
  4. Want your real Gemini account (with its history and your logged-in features)? Click “Open full Gemini ↗” in the panel header.
  5. Close the panel with the ✕, the Escape key, or by clicking outside it. Use “Clear chat” to wipe the conversation on this device.

Add a bookmark while reading an article

  1. While on the article, click the + Add to StartDashboard bookmarklet in your bookmarks bar.
  2. A small popup window appears. You see all your personas grouped, with category buttons under each.
  3. Click the category where the bookmark should go. The window closes automatically.
  4. If your main dashboard tab is viewing a different persona than the one you added to, a banner appears at the top: "Just added X to Personal — Switch to Personal." Click Switch to jump there.

Add a description to a bookmark

  1. Hover the bookmark and click the 📝 icon.
  2. A modal opens with a multi-line text area. Type as much as you want — reminders, line breaks, asterisks for your own notes.
  3. Click Save (or Cmd+Enter). A small accent dot appears next to the bookmark's title.
  4. To read it later, hover the bookmark for half a second — a popover appears with the full text.

Set up the Markets / Watchlist widgets

  1. Open Settings ⚙ on the dashboard. Scroll to the Finnhub API key section.
  2. Follow the inline walkthrough: sign up at finnhub.io/register (free, no credit card, 60 calls/min), copy your key from their dashboard, paste it in.
  3. Click Save changes. The Markets widget should start showing prices within a few seconds.
  4. Click the Markets ⚙ to add tickers (defaults include SPY, QQQ, DIA). Watchlist works the same way for individual stocks like AAPL, NVDA.
  5. Note: as of v1.9 the dashboard uses Finnhub, not Twelve Data — the switch fixed the rate-limit errors that appeared with longer symbol lists. If you had an old Twelve Data key saved, it's simply ignored; paste a Finnhub key to get prices back.

Switch to light mode for evening reading (v1.6)

  1. Click the ☀ icon in the header (it sits next to the gear).
  2. The whole dashboard fades to a light background, dark text, and white cards. The persona's accent color is preserved.
  3. To go back to dark, click the 🌙 icon (the button glyph flipped when you went light).
  4. Your choice is remembered per browser — reloading keeps the theme you picked, and another browser/device can stay on the opposite theme.

Switch from Work to Personal mode

  1. Click the Personal tab in the top bar.
  2. The whole page re-renders: different bookmarks, different widgets, different background, different accent color.
  3. Switch back to Work the same way.

Set up StartDashboard on a new browser

  1. Open https://tecgunther.github.io/startdashboard/ in the new browser.
  2. Generate a new GitHub Personal Access Token (one per browser — never share tokens).
  3. Paste the token into the setup screen.
  4. Choose I already set up another browser, paste the gist ID from your first browser (found in Settings → Sync), and continue.
  5. The dashboard loads with all your data. Optionally set this browser's default persona in Settings.

Create a new persona for a focused area (e.g. Investments)

  1. Click the + button next to the persona tabs.
  2. Type a name for the persona ("Investments") and click OK.
  3. A fresh dashboard appears with one empty category. Start adding categories and bookmarks.
  4. Open Settings (gear icon) to customize the accent color and background for this persona.

Reorder your widget cards (v1.10)

  1. Find the ⠿ grip handle in the header of the widget card you want to move.
  2. Click and hold it, then drag the card left or right. A blue line shows where it will land.
  3. Release. The new widget order saves automatically and sticks across reloads and your other browsers.

Add a second watchlist (v1.10)

  1. Open Settings ⚙ and scroll to the widget show/hide toggles. Click “+ Add watchlist.”
  2. A new, empty watchlist card appears (default-titled, e.g. “Watchlist 2”). Click its ⚙ gear.
  3. Rename it in the title field — the card’s title updates as you type. Add tickers (type a symbol or search), and they start showing prices.
  4. To remove a watchlist later, open its ⚙ gear and click Delete this watchlist (you’ll be asked to confirm).

Recolor a card (v1.10)

  1. For a category: click its “...” menu and choose “Card color…”. For a widget: click its ⚙ gear and find the “Card color” section (To-do and Notes have a gear just for this).
  2. Pick a preset swatch, or use the color picker for a custom hex. The card’s border and soft glow update right away.
  3. To clear it, choose “Default (theme)” — the card returns to the persona’s accent color. The color is saved per-persona.

Reorder items inside a box (v1.9)

  1. Find the ⠿ grip handle at the left of the item you want to move — it's on categories, bookmarks, and the lists in widget config (stock tickers, weather cities, clock zones).
  2. Click and hold the grip, then drag up or down. A blue line shows where the item will drop.
  3. Release. The new order saves automatically and sticks across reloads and your other browsers.

Reorganize bookmarks across categories

  1. Grab the bookmark's ⠿ grip handle and drag it onto another category card; drop on the top or bottom half of a row to place it before or after that bookmark.
  2. The bookmark moves immediately. Sync saves the change ~2 seconds later.
  3. To move a bookmark to a different persona, hover the bookmark and click the ↗ icon, then pick the target persona and category.

Add a city to the weather widget

  1. Click the ⚙ icon on the Weather widget card (each widget has its own gear).
  2. Type a city name in the search box (e.g. “San Francisco”) and pick a match from the dropdown. Coordinates are stored invisibly — no need to look up lat/lon.
  3. The widget refreshes with the new city immediately. Clock works the same way for time zones (city → IANA timezone lookup via the same Open-Meteo endpoint).

Intended Audience

Who this app is built for and what level of expertise it assumes.

StartDashboard is built for one person: Tom. It assumes you have a GitHub account, can copy-paste a personal access token, and can run two git commands to push the file to GitHub Pages. No code-writing is required once setup is done.

It's not designed for multi-user use, team sharing, or distribution as a product. Other people could fork it and run their own copy with their own gist, but there's no admin layer, user accounts, or sharing model.

What This App Isn't

The scope boundary — what people might assume this does that it doesn't.

  • Not a bookmark manager replacement — the browser's own bookmarks still exist; StartDashboard is a curated overlay you choose what goes into.
  • Not an automatic browser-bookmark import tool — bookmarks come over manually as you use the bookmarklet. A JSON-import flow could be added later if needed.
  • Not multi-user. One person, one gist.
  • Not real-time. Sync polls every 60 seconds in the background; saves are debounced about 2 seconds. Edit in two browsers at once and the most recent save wins.
  • Not a notes app. The Notes widget is a scratchpad, not a place to write long documents.
  • Not a task manager. The To-do widget is a quick checklist, not a project tracker.
  • Not a widget marketplace — the built-in widget types (Clock, Weather, To-do, Notes, Crypto, Markets, and Watchlists) are baked into the file. You can have multiple watchlists, but new widget types can only be added by editing index.html directly.
  • Not a browser extension. The bookmarklet does the same job without per-browser build overhead.
  • Not a mobile-native app. The web page is responsive and works on mobile Safari; there's no App Store version.
